Visit a new city and fall in love, just like in the movies!

Ever been to a new city and wanted to visit all the sites you've watched in your favourite movie? In just one short weekend? "Lov-In London" brings to you a chance to get swept off your feet and travel to all the local hotspots visited in 3 infamous films shot in London!

A little bit about us.

Born out of the Code First: Girls 2018 group, we come from Spain and work in Digital and Investment Banking industries. We're curious, passionate about innovation and really excited to be able to showcase our learnings through this website! Hope our website makes you feel like you're a part of these films themselves!

Notting Hill. Get swept off your feet on our first tour.

1. Portobello Road: The heart of the film is Notting Hill’s Portobello Road (Portobello Rd, London W11 1AN)

2. William Thacker’s House: Famous for the blue door (280 Westbourne Park Rd, London W11 1EH)

3. The Travel Bookshop: William’s book store in Notting Hill (142 Portobello Rd, London W11 2DZ)
4. The Ritz: Where Anna Scott stays and William passes himself off as a reporter from Horse and Hound to meet her. (150 Piccadilly, London W1J 9BR)

Bridget Jones's Diary. From writing to finding the one.

1. Bridget’s flat: is above The Globe, Bedale Street at Green Dragon Court (8 Bedale St, London SE1 9AL)

2. Borough Market: Bridget plods home (8 Southwark St, London SE1 1TL)

3. The smart flat of Daniel Cleaver: one of the smart warehouse redevelopments only a minute or two away from Bridget’s place (Clink Wharf Apartments, Clink Street, London SE1)
4. Restaurant Momo: Before the first date with Daniel, Bridget gets advice from her friends at fashionable, exotic and expensive Maghreb restaurant ( 25-27 Heddon St, London W1B 4BH)

Four Weddings and a Funeral. Checkmate.

1. Charles’ apartment: on the northwest corner of Highbury Fields at the junction of Highbury Terrace Mews, N5 (22 Highbury Terrace, London N5 1UP)

2. Wedding No. 2: Bernard and Lydia got married at the Catholic St Mary of the Fields and was filmed in the Anglican Old Royal Naval College Chapel ( King William Walk, Greenwich, London SE10 9NN)

3. National Film Theatre: Charles hares off to meet his brother at the entrance to the old National Film Theatre, and he explains to Carrie about David Cassidy and the Partridge Family (Upper Ground, Lambeth, London SE1 9PX)
4. Non-wedding No. 4: The church where Charles has second thoughts (St Bartholomew the Great Cloth Fair, London EC1A 7JQ)
